About Me

Doctoral student in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at Johns Hopkins University [Baltimore, USA], working in the Laboratory of Computational Audio Perception under the advisement of Professor Mounya Elhilali. I graduated from Vellore Institute of Technology [Vellore, India] with a B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering in June 2020. My interest lies in exploring the domain of Artificial Intelligence [AI] and Brain Inspired Computational Models for Audio. As a part of my final undergrad project, I investigated applications of Deep Learning in Communication Systems. After graduating, I worked at a generative AI startup [DeepSync] and acquired valuable research experience and skills of working with real time modelling of audio for podcast production.

My present research focuses on inventing and investigating auditory perception inspired neural networks for tasks such as Speech Enhancement and Source Seperation. In my free time, I love to paint and play with mixing music.

Feel free to contact me if you are interested in my research or have any questions. I am more than happy to hear from you!
